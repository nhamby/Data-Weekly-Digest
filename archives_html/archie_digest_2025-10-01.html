Data Brokers: How Your Personal Data Becomes Business

Source: CNET

Data brokers collect information from online activity, purchases, and public records for resale. This industry generates billions annually by creating detailed consumer profiles for marketing. Data breaches pose risks, as 40% of Americans were affected by one in the past year.

The Trade Desk launches new AI tool to overhaul costly third - party data buying | Advertising

Source: campaignasia.com

The Trade Desk's new AI, Kokai, aims to reduce reliance on expensive third-party data by leveraging first-party data for targeting. Kokai's simulated environments help advertisers identify high-potential audiences, reportedly improving campaign performance by 30%. This shift could save companies significant costs currently allocated to less effective third-party data sources.

Challenge to EU - US Data Transfers Fails | Ius Laboris

Source: jdsupra.com

The EU-US Data Privacy Framework validity was upheld, enabling transatlantic data flows to continue legally. Transfers rely on self-certification, demanding continuous compliance monitoring to avoid violations. Organizations face potential GDPR scrutiny on transfers, requiring diligent risk assessments for data protection.

A Guide to Data Removal Services: Should You Pay for Privacy?

Source: CNET

Data removal services help reclaim privacy by scrubbing data from brokers, costing time or money. DIY removal saves money but requires consistent effort, potentially missing some broker sites. Paid services automate removal from many brokers, claiming 80-90% success, but may not eliminate all data.
