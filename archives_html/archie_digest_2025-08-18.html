HM Courts and Tribunals Service Data Access Panel

Source: Www.gov.uk

The HMCTS Data Access Panel decides on third-party access requests to HMCTS data for public interest research. The panel's focus is enabling research using HMCTS data, but protecting sensitive information. Data access decisions are based on public benefit and risks related to privacy and security.

Data brokers just gave us another reason to hate them

Source: 9to5Mac

Data brokers profit immensely by purchasing and reselling personal data obtained from apps and websites. The data fuels spam and targeted advertising, raising significant privacy concerns for consumers. This highlights a crucial need for ethical data practices, differentiating startups from predatory brokers.

Why some companies are making it extra difficult to delete your data

Source: Biztoc.com

California law mandates data brokers provide data deletion options, yet access proves elusive. Over thirty data companies collect and sell consumer data, making deletion difficult. Successfully deleting data required many attempts, with some companies ignoring requests entirely, defying CCPA.

States Issue Guidance on Adviser Use of Data Aggregation Tools

Source: Regulationtomorrow.com

Texas and New Jersey issued guidance for investment advisers using data aggregation tools. The guidance addresses due diligence obligations for selecting and monitoring third-party platforms. Advisers must implement cybersecurity policies and regularly audit data security; a critical factor.

Incogni vs . Optery : Which Data Removal Service Comes Out on Top ?

Source: pcmag.com

Incogni edges Optery in data broker removal, eliminating 165 brokers versus Optery's 155 in testing. Incogni's streamlined approach offers a slightly simpler user experience, especially during initial setup and ongoing monitoring. Optery's free plan and tiered subscriptions provide greater flexibility, though Incogni focuses solely on comprehensive paid protection.
